History of TechCon

TechCon, organized by the Society of Vacuum Coaters (SVC), has a rich history as a premier event for innovation and excellence in the semiconductor industry and realted coating technologies. Since its inception, it has served as a vital platform for researchers, industry professionals, and students to gather, exchange ideas, and celebrate advancements.

Originally conceived to foster collaboration and knowledge sharing, TechCon has evolved to address teh dynamic landscape of materials science, vacuum coating, and semiconductor development. The conference consistently features cutting-edge research, practical applications, and discussions on future trends that shape technological progress globally.

Our Mission

TechCon's core mission is to serve as leading platform for exchanging ideas, fostering collaboration, and celebrating achievements within the semiconductor industry and broader vacuum coating community. We are dedicated to driving technological advancements and promoting sustainable solutions that make a significant impact on the world.

We aim to bring together a deverse group of industry leaders, undergraduate and graduate students, and technical recruiters, creating a rich and vibrant atmosphere for collaborative learning. Through insightful presentations, interactive sessions, and unparalled networking opportunities, TechCon strives to propel the industry forward and inspire the next generation of innovators.

Notable Past Speakers

Deirdre Hanford

CEO of Natcast and recipient of the 2025 IEEE Frederik Philips Award. Deirdre delivered a keynote titled "Future Forward: How R&D will Continue to Propel the Semiconductor Industry." She emphasized the human element in technology, discussed global supply chain complexities, energy consumption, and the impact of the Chips and Science Act. She also shared valuable career advice on embracing lateral moves for professional growth.

Katie Yu

Senior Director of Package Innovation for Automotive Processors at NXP Semiconductors. Katie's energetic talk, "Looking Ahead - Secure Edge/Automotive Package Technology Opportunities," covered the importance of collaboration, cost-effectiveness, and environmental impact in advancing electrification and autonomous driving technologies.
